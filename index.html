I am an Informatics student at the University of Washington. I never expected to end up working in the technology field, and I definitely never anticipated being as passionate about it as I am. During my time at UW, I have immersed myself in programming classes, classes on information ethics, and information systems analysis and management. In addition to my major, I am working towards a math minor as well as an art history minor. As a minority in the tech field, I am a huge advocate in diversifying the workforce. However, I don't believe this should be done through contrived hiring practices, I prefer to work at the problem from a different angle. I have participated in several community events geared towards getting children, particularly young girls interested in technology and computing. There is so much this field has to offer, and so many people who may miss out on it because they weren't exposed to the computational way of thinking early enough to pursue it.

I am a Washingtonian by birth and a Seattleite by choice. I love a good cup of coffee, rain boots, dystopian lit, and living in a city that is so outdoorsy. When I have downtime (which is rare and precious), I generally go running, visit the farmers market, read, and do crosswords.
